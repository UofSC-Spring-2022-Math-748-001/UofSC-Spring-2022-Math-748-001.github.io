Identities, projections, and convolutions

Let's expand out analogy with integral transforms in analysis a bit more. There are three nice properties of such linear transformations:

  1. Identity kernels. Let Δ={(x,x)∈X×X} be the diagonal. Then, ∫X​δΔ​(x,y)f(x) dx=f(y) for the δ-function along Δ.
  2. Projection kernels. For a kernel of the form K(x,y)=e(x)h(y), we have ∫X​K(x,y)f(x) dx=h(y)∫X​e(x)f(x) dx is a type of projection onto h(y).
  3. Composition as convolution. For K(x,y) and Q(y,z) the composition of f(x)↦∫X​K(x,y)f(x) dx and f(y)↦∫Y​Q(y,z)f(y) dy is also an integral transformation with kernel Q⋆K:=∫Y​K(x,y)Q(y,z) dy

These three properties still hold integral transforms of derived categories.
